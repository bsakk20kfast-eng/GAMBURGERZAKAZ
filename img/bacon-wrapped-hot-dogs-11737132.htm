Bacon-Wrapped Hot Dogs

Be the first to rate & review!

Give your typical cookout food a bacon twist!

By
Josh Miller
Josh Miller
Josh Miller is a writer, editor, recipe developer, and food stylist who has been writing about Southern food and working in the publishing industry for the past 20 years. His work has appeared in Southern Living, Food & Wine, Cooking Light, Taste of the South, and Southern Cast Iron magazines.
Southern Living's editorial guidelines
Published on June 25, 2025
Active Time:
30 mins
Total Time:
30 mins
Servings:
8

Some folks might consider it a bit indulgent to wrap a hot dog in bacon, but here in the South, it suits us just fine. While thick-cut bacon is great for breakfast or as a burger topping, in this recipe we prefer center-cut bacon. Its smaller size and thinner cut makes it just the right size for wrapping around hot dogs.

To get the bacon crispy, use your favoritecast-iron skillet, and turn the bacon-wrapped dogs occasionally to help all the sides get nice and crispy. While store-bought is absolutely fine, we recommend making homemade pico de gallo for a super-fresh garnish for these over-the-top dogs.

Ingredients

  • 1 (10-oz.) pkg. bun-length all-beef hot dogs (such as Nathans Famous)

  • 8 bacon slices(not thick-cut)

  • 1 cup pico de gallo

  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro

  • 8 brioche hot dog buns, toasted or steamed

  • Yellow mustard

  • Pickled jalapenos

Directions

  1. Gather all ingredients.

  2. Tightly wrap each hot dog in a slice of bacon spiraling from end to end, stretching slightly until hot dog is completely wrapped.

  3. Place hot dogs seam-side down in a large cast-iron skillet over medium; cook, turning occasionally, until bacon is crispy all over and rendered, about 18 minutes.

  4. Meanwhile, stir together pico de gallo and cilantro in a medium bowl; set aside.

  5. Place a bacon-wrapped hot dog in each bun, and drizzle with mustard.

    Top evenly with pico de gallo mixture and pickled jalapeos.
